General Corpus & N-Gram Documentation

Our Corpus

The text used to generate n-grams and networks are sourced from the EarlyPrint lab, and have been further cleaned, standardized and corrected. EarlyPrint has already processed the text with some spelling correction and lemmatization. In addition to this, stopwords are left in the corpus (e.g. 'the' or 'and'), but all words that are two characters or less have been removed (except for 'if' and 'of'). Common misspellings of important terms relating to trade and commodities have been corrected (e.g. 'spaine' is corrected to 'spain'). In addition, all punctuation has been removed, and any missing characters are denoted with a '^'. This process helps to maximize our analysis of n-grams, but it also means that the context windows provided in the documentation may not make grammatical sense. They do, however, give a sense of the discourse that a particular commodity was involved in. Given that the documentation includes their TCP IDs, it is convenient to search up the original documents in EEBO.

Standardizer dictionaries for common spelling variations and missing char. words can be found here.

Opium N-Gram Generation

The opium n-grams in this network are created from Gensim's bigram and trigram models. We found all the n-grams that include the following terms: opium, opiate, poppy, poppey, and laudanum. Some 'trigrams' have four words because we have deliberately ignored connecting words like 'of' when counting the value of 'n'. Scroll to the Text Information section in each network's documentation file for the word contexts around the n-grams occurring in each text.
